Place Ontologies and Semantic Place Modelling

This research programme investigates the formal representation and semantic modelling of geographic place — extending beyond traditional gazetteers to capture the rich, multidimensional nature of how humans understand, experience, and interact with places. Spanning over 15 years, this work has developed ontologies and computational models that represent place affordance, qualitative spatial relationships, user perceptions, and social semantics of place.

The research addresses fundamental questions: What activities and services does a place afford? How do people qualitatively describe place location? How can we integrate formal place knowledge with vernacular understanding from social media? How can place semantics be represented on the Linked Data Web and in GIS systems?

Research Themes

  • Place Affordance: Modelling the human activities, services, and functions associated with geographic place types — what places enable people to do.
  • Qualitative Spatial Representation: Representing place location using qualitative expressions (near, north of, inside) rather than purely coordinate-based approaches.
  • Geo-Folksonomies: Extracting place semantics from collaborative tagging and social media, capturing vernacular and experiential views of place.
  • Linked Data and Knowledge Graphs: Representing place information on the Semantic Web, enabling integration and reasoning across distributed place resources.
  • User and Place Profiling: Building profiles of places and users from location-based social network data, supporting personalised location services.
  • GIS Integration: Implementing qualitative place models in Geographic Information Systems, including integration with Discrete Global Grid Systems (DGGS).

Key Contributions

  • Qualitative place models for the Linked Data Web enabling spatial reasoning
  • QPM-QGIS: Qualitative place modelling integrated with Geographic Information Systems
  • The DLIG ontology pattern for global place knowledge graphs
  • GIS-native frameworks with DGGS integration for qualitative place representation
  • User and place profiling methods for location-based social networks
